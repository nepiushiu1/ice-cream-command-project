How it’s made?

Commercial ice cream is made with six steps: Milk, cream, milk solids, sugars, modifying agents and flavourings are blended together in stainless steel vats. The mix is homogenised to create a consistent texture. The mix is pasteurised by heating at 82-85°C for 15 seconds then cooled to destroy bacteria. The mix is left alone for 2-24 hours so fat globules solidify and viscosity increases. Flavours or colourings are added. The mix is frozen (at about -7°C) and beaten (or ‘agitated’). This adds air to the mix.

The ice cream is moulded, packaged and frozen at around -25°C to allow it to harden.
